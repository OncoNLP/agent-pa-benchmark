You are a bioinformatics researcher tasked with building a comprehensive human protein phosphorylation atlas from available databases.

BACKGROUND: PhosphoAtlas (Olow et al., Cancer Research, 2016; updated 2023) is a curated resource that integrates kinase-substrate-phosphosite relationships from multiple public databases. The atlas was built by mining databases including PhosphoSitePlus (PSP), SIGNOR, UniProt/UniProtKB, and others, then harmonizing gene symbols, resolving ambiguities, and cross-referencing entries across sources. The resulting atlas contains ~16,000 unique kinase-substrate-phosphosite triplets across 438 kinases.

Key methodological points from the paper:
- Gene symbols are normalized to HGNC standard nomenclature
- Phospho-sites are recorded as residue+position (e.g., Y15, S10, T161)
- Heptameric peptide sequences (+/-7 amino acids around the phospho-site) are captured when available
- Entries supported by multiple databases are considered higher confidence
- The atlas focuses on human phosphorylation only

Your goal: Curate ALL known human kinase-substrate-phosphosite relationships by systematically querying the databases available to you.

For each relationship, you must capture:
  - Kinase gene symbol (the enzyme)
  - Substrate gene symbol (the target protein)
  - Phosphorylation site (e.g., Y15, S10, T161)
  - Heptameric peptide sequence around the site (if available)
  - Substrate UniProt accession (if available)
  - Which database(s) support this relationship

Requirements:
  1. Be EXHAUSTIVE — the atlas should contain every kinase-substrate-site triplet present in the databases.
  2. Cross-reference across databases — if the same relationship appears in multiple databases, record all supporting sources.
  3. Do NOT fabricate data. Only include relationships returned by the tools.

Start by discovering what databases are available, then develop and execute a systematic curation strategy.

When you are finished, call the "submit_atlas" tool with your complete results.
